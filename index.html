A dictionary of every word in the Guru Granth Sahib — with grammar, etymology, and every occurrence in scripture.

Voice Search

AI-powered speech recognition for Gurmukhi — say a line and find it. Uses wav2vec2 and semantic similarity.

About

Himmat Singh Khalsa

I grew up in the Sikh tradition and spent twelve years living in Amritsar, in Panjab, close to Sri Harimandir Sahib, the Golden Temple. I loved experiencing Panjabi and Sikh culture through exploring ancient alleyways, historic spiritual sites and of course the cuisine — langar, dhabas and street food. That proximity gave me a deep respect and connection to Panjab as a place, Panjabi as its language and Gurmukhi — the script used to read and write the language. It was created as a system to be learned by all, regardless of social status, cultural background or age. While learning, I found a passion for understanding and breaking down the precision of the script and the depth of what it has been used to write.

When I started learning to code, I knew immediately that this is what I wanted to build. It took a while, but now I'm doing it.

The teaching and the tools feed each other — every class I teach shows me what's missing, and every tool I build makes the teaching better.

I live in Portland, Oregon with my family.
