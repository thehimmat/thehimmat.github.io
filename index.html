I work at the intersection of language and technology, building tools that make Sikh scripture more accessible through search, voice, and scholarly analysis.

Multi-mode search across scripture: substring, first-letter, regex, and phonetic pattern matching.

Gurmukhi Kosh

A dictionary of every word in the Guru Granth Sahib, with grammar, etymology, and every occurrence in scripture.

About

Himmat Singh Khalsa

I was born into a Sikh household and was learning Gurmukhī from fridge magnets before I could read English. From Sikh camps in Canada in my early years and home Kīrtan programs followed by home cooked langar, I finally arrived in Panjab at age 11. Reading and writing came naturally and I spent twelve years living and learning in Amritsar, in Panjab, close to Sri Harimandir Sahib, the Golden Temple. I loved experiencing Panjabi and Sikh culture through exploring ancient alleyways, historic spiritual sites and of course the cuisine: langar, dhabas and street food. That proximity gave me a deep respect and connection to Panjab as a place, Panjabi as its language and Gurmukhi, the script used to read and write the language. It was created as a system to be learned by all, regardless of social status, cultural background or age. While learning, I found a passion for understanding and breaking down the precision of the script and the depth of what it has been used to write.

When I started learning to code, I knew immediately that this is what I wanted to build. It took a while, but now I'm doing it.

The teaching and the tools feed each other. Every class I teach shows me what's missing, and every tool I build makes the teaching better.

I live in Portland, Oregon with my family.
